## 3. Where to Apply (Beyond LinkedIn)

LinkedIn is only one channel. Diversify so you’re not dependent on it.

### Job boards and aggregators
- **Indeed** — Huge volume; apply to roles that match your title and skills; set “San Francisco Bay Area” or “Remote (US)”.
- **Glassdoor** — Jobs + company reviews; use to screen culture and interview process.
- **Wellfound (formerly AngelList)** — Startups; often more open to non-linear backgrounds and career returns.
- **Built In** — Tech and startup jobs; filter by San Francisco Bay Area.
- **FlexJobs** — If you want remote or flexible options; curated, fewer scams.

### Bay Area–focused and niche
- **Hired.com** — You create a profile; companies reach out. Strong for tech-adjacent roles (PM, SM, TPM).
- **Underdog.io** — Startup-focused; good for PM/ops at early-stage companies.
- **Company career pages** — Apply directly on **careers.***company*.com for any target company. Often fewer applicants than LinkedIn Easy Apply.

### Referrals (highest impact)
- **Alumni:** ICFAI, Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam Technical University, ZS, Sun Life, HCL — find people in Bay Area or at target companies on LinkedIn and ask for a short call or referral.
- **PM communities:** Bay Area PM meetups (Meetup.com), Women in Product, PMI San Francisco Bay Area chapter. Engage first (events, comments), then ask for advice and referrals.
- **Former managers and colleagues** — Ask for referrals to their network or company, especially in the US or remote-US roles.

### Recruiters and staffing
- **Robert Half, Randstad, TEKsystems, Aston Carter** — Often have PM/Scrum Master/PMO contracts or full-time roles; get on their radar with a clear “I’m looking for PM/Scrum Master/PMO in Bay Area or remote.”
- **Pharma/life sciences:** **Kelly Scientific, Lab Support, Yoh** — For pharma PM/analyst roles given your ZS background.

## 4. How to Get More Traction (Why LinkedIn Might Feel Stuck)

- **Volume and noise:** LinkedIn Easy Apply = hundreds of applicants; many get lost.
- **ATS and keywords:** If your resume doesn’t match job title and key terms (e.g. “Project Manager,” “JIRA,” “Agile,” “Scrum”), you may be filtered out before a human sees it.
- **Algorithm and activity:** Profiles that get engagement (posts, comments, profile updates) sometimes get more visibility.

### What to do differently
1. **Apply on company career sites** for roles you find on LinkedIn or Indeed — often a smaller applicant pool.
2. **Use your resume–job analyzer** — For each serious application, paste the job description and your resume and tighten the resume (and cover letter) to match keywords and themes.
3. **Get 1–2 referrals per week** — One referred application often beats 50 cold applications.
4. **Short, tailored cover notes** — When you can attach a note, 2–3 lines: “I’m a PM with 3+ years in pharma and financial services, CSM and SAFe, and recent independent work in AI and process optimization. I’d like to bring this to [Company]’s [team/role].”
5. **Refresh LinkedIn** — Add “Open to work” (visible to recruiters only if you prefer), refresh Headline and About to include “Project Manager | Scrum Master | Agile” and your Independent Projects/AI angle.

## 5. SF Bay Area Job Market — What to Expect

- **Competition:** High, especially for big tech and household names. Mid-size companies, startups, pharma, and consulting often have fewer applicants and more openness to career returns.
- **Remote vs local:** Many Bay Area companies offer hybrid or remote. “Remote (US)” expands your options beyond San Mateo.
- **Contract vs FTE:** Contract or contract-to-hire is common; it can be a way back in and then convert.
- **Salary range (rough):** PM/Scrum Master in Bay Area can range from ~$90k–$120k (mid/small) to $120k–$160k+ (larger tech). Use Glassdoor/Levels.fyi/PMI salary data for your target title and industry.
